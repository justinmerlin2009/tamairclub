Innovation in Action

Discover how we're using technology to protect our environment

3D Printed UAV

Our flagship project involves designing and manufacturing affordable unmanned aerial vehicles using 3D printing technology. These drones are equipped with high-resolution cameras for environmental monitoring.

  • Custom-designed airframes for optimal flight performance
  • Camera systems for aerial photography and mapping
  • Extended flight time for comprehensive surveys
  • Cost-effective manufacturing process

FPV Drone Operations

First-person view drones allow our pilots to navigate through challenging terrain with precision. These agile aircraft are perfect for detailed inspections and search operations.

  • Real-time video transmission
  • Highly maneuverable for tight spaces
  • HD recording capabilities
  • VR headset integration for immersive control

Learning Log

Documenting our flight tests, challenges, and breakthroughs
May 4, 2025

Flight 1: Foam Plane Practice

Team: Justin, Michel, Olivier
Location: Near Petaluma

Sunny conditions with strong westerly gusts (12-20 mph). Foam plane crashed into barbed wire. FPV electronics overheated. Learned valuable lessons about wind conditions and equipment preparation.
May 31, 2025

Flight 2: Lark Takeoff & Landing

Team: Justin, Gideon, Dante, Olivier
Location: Open pasture, Petaluma

Sunny, calm winds (~3 mph). Used ANGL mode (angle stabilization). Successful loop and landing with no damage! Initial oscillations noted for future calibration.
June 7, 2025

Flight 3: Three Complete Flights!

Team: Justin, Olivier
Location: Open pasture, Petaluma

Overcast, light westerly wind 5-8 mph. Completed 3 flights (~5 min each) in ACRO mode with smooth ascents and landings. Learned FAA requires staying below 400 ft without special waiver.
July 6, 2025

Flight 4: Hill Takeoffs

Team: Justin, Olivier
Location: Open pasture, Petaluma

Clear skies, small gusts (5-8 mph). ~6 minute flight. Discovered calibration issue affecting left turns. Planning second LARK backup, Stallion with gimbal camera, and Moose payload carrier.
