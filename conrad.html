What is the Conrad Challenge?

The Conrad Challenge is a multi-phase innovation and entrepreneurial competition for students ages 13-18. Named after Apollo 12 astronaut Charles "Pete" Conrad, the competition encourages students to develop innovative products and services that address real-world challenges.

The competition focuses on four key categories: Aerospace & Aviation, Cyber-Technology & Security, Energy & Environment, and Health & Nutrition. Teams work to create sustainable solutions with genuine market potential.

Visit Conrad Challenge

Competition Categories

  • Aerospace & Aviation - Innovations in flight and space technology
  • Cyber-Technology & Security - Digital solutions and cybersecurity
  • Energy & Environment - Our category! Sustainable solutions for environmental challenges
  • Health & Nutrition - Healthcare and wellness innovations

Introducing Sylva

Our Conrad Challenge entry for environmental monitoring

Sylva

Every year, 8-14 million tons of plastic enter our oceans—killing over 100,000 marine animals.

California is fighting back, but cleanup teams need better tools to know where to focus.

Sylva delivers actionable intelligence from the air—500 acres per hour.

Visit Sylva Website

The Problem

California's highways and coastlines accumulate millions of pounds of litter annually. Cleanup organizations struggle to efficiently allocate resources because they don't know where pollution hotspots are located.

  • Millions of pounds of litter along highways yearly
  • Cleanup teams work without data on hotspots
  • Manual surveys are time-consuming and incomplete
  • Resources often misallocated to cleaner areas

Our Solution

Sylva is an environmental monitoring dashboard that uses drone imagery and AI to detect and map litter along highways and coastlines, helping organizations like Caltrans and Keep California Beautiful prioritize cleanup efforts.

  • Drone-based aerial imagery collection
  • AI-powered litter detection and mapping
  • Interactive dashboard for cleanup teams
  • Data-driven resource allocation

Why This Matters

The Conrad Challenge isn't just a competition - it's an opportunity to turn our vision into reality. Winning or placing highly would provide resources, mentorship, and visibility to scale our environmental monitoring solution.

Beyond the competition, participation validates our approach and connects us with other young innovators working on similar challenges. The skills we develop - entrepreneurship, engineering, teamwork - will serve us regardless of outcome.
